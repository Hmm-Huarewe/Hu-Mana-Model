Hu-Mana Language · HML

The shared vocabulary.
One word. Three readings.

The HML is the small set of terms that lets science, spirituality, and philosophy describe one reality without translation wars. Each term has an everyday meaning for readability, a simple symbol for formula use, and three readings — one per leg — so that every tradition can find itself in the language without anyone having to abandon their own ground.

Why this language exists

Science calls it a field.
Spirituality calls it presence.
Philosophy calls it ground of being.
They are all pointing at the same thing.

The problem is not that these traditions disagree. The problem is that they have never had a shared vocabulary — so a physicist and a meditator can spend an hour in conversation and never realize they are describing the same phenomenon from opposite sides of the same field.

The HML is the minimum number of shared terms required for science, spirituality, and philosophy to recognize each other in the same room. Once you know these letters — F, E, M, RITE, FEMisSphere, CU, ICU, CW, L-Wave — you will begin to see them everywhere you already look.

The goal is not to bury anyone in jargon. It is to give just enough shared language that when the model says CU or FEMisSphere, every reader — scientist, practitioner, philosopher — can feel exactly what is meant. This is the legend of the map. The place you can always return to when a symbol feels fuzzy or a term begins to blur. The LOI is the law. The HML is the language the law speaks in.
